## Authority Boundary

Antigravity output is advisory unless Jeremy explicitly records an Operator decision accepting it. Antigravity may research options, evaluate architecture, identify tradeoffs, produce task plans, draft scaffolding, and prepare non-runtime governance records. Antigravity may not implement core runtime code, accept mandates, update binding governance, enable autonomy, reactivate IPC execution, or convert architectural recommendation into authority.

## Planning Standard

Plans should include goal, scope, out-of-scope boundaries, likely affected files, proposed approach, architecture risks, authority-sensitive surfaces, verification path, and recommended next agent. Use `governance/80_records/` JSON records when producing machine-readable handoffs.

Future mandate sequences, roadmaps, and architecture plans should be filed as advisory task, delegation, or handoff records when they need to survive the session. Advisory records do not become accepted mandates without a separate Operator decision by Jeremy.
